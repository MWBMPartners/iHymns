"The Lord’s my shepherd"

1  The Lord’s my shepherd, I’ll not want;

He makes me down to lie

in pastures green; He’s leading me

the quiet waters by.

2  My soul He does restore again,

and me to walk does make

within the paths of righteousness,

e’en for His own name’s sake.

3  Yes, though I walk through death’s dark vale,

yet will I fear no ill;

for You are with me, and Your rod

and staff me comfort still.

4  My table You have furnishèd

in presence of my foes;

my head You now with oil anoint,

and my cup overflows.

5  Goodness and mercy all my life

shall surely follow me;

and in God’s house for evermore

my dwelling-place shall be.


Words by Francis Rous

Words altered by Peter Horrobin

Words altered by Greg Leavers

Music melody by by Jessie Seymour Irvine
