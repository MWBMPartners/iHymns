"As water to the thirsty"

1  As water to the thirsty,

as beauty to the eyes,

as strength that follows weakness,

as truth instead of lies,

as songtime and springtime

and summertime to be,

so is my Lord,

my living Lord,

so is my Lord to me.

2  Like calm in place of clamour,

like peace that follows pain,

like meeting after parting,

like sunshine after rain,

like moonlight and starlight

and sunlight on the sea,

so is my Lord,

my living Lord,

so is my Lord to me.

3  As sleep that follows fever,

as gold instead of grey,

as freedom after bondage,

as sunrise to the day;

as home to the traveller

and all we long to see,

so is my Lord,

my living Lord,

so is my Lord to me.


Words by Timothy Dudley-Smith

Music by T Brian Coleman

Music arranged by Roger Mayor
