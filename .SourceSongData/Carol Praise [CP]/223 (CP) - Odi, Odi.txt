"Odi, Odi"

1  We’re told he was born at a Bethlehem inn,

where Joseph and Mary had begged to get in;

for birth-place a stable was all they could find

although he was God’s gift to all of our kind.

‘Odi, odi!’ a stranger has come!

‘Odi, odi! Will you welcome me here?’

‘Odi, odi! I stand here and wait.’

‘Odi, odi! Will you open your gate?’

2  He comes now – a stranger who asks for our aid,

he comes in disguise that he might persuade;

he comes to us early, he comes to us late,

and humbly he waits and he cries at our gate.

‘Odi, odi...’

3  He comes as a servant to black and to white,

and shows us the way that we all may unite;

he offers his friendship to rich and to poor,

he calls to each one as he stands at the door.

‘Odi, odi!’ a stranger has come!

‘Odi, odi! Will you welcome me here?’

‘Odi, odi! I stand here and wait.’

‘Odi, odi! Will you open your gate?’

4  He comes as a brother, he comes as a friend,

he comes all our hatred and warfare to end;

he comes to unite and he comes to restore,

but – always so humbly – he calls at our door.

‘Odi, odi...’

5  Lord Jesus, you’re welcome, the door’s open wide;

we beg you to enter and with us abide:

your love in our heart and your smile on our face,

we’ll see you in strangers who stand in your place.

‘Odi, odi...’

‘Odini, odini! Believers out there!’

‘Odini, odini! We do welcome you here.’

‘Odini, odini! Please enter – don’t wait.’

‘Odini, odini! We have opened our gate!’


Words by Tom Colvin
